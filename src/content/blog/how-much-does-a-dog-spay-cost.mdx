A dog spay (ovariohysterectomy) is one of the most common veterinary procedures in the United States, but the price range is surprisingly wide. Depending on where you live, the size of your dog, and the type of clinic you visit, you might pay anywhere from $200 at a low-cost spay/neuter clinic to $800 or more at a full-service veterinary practice in a high cost-of-living city.

The biggest driver of cost isn't the surgery itself — it's what's included. A $250 quote might mean the procedure only, while a $500 quote from a different clinic might bundle pre-surgical bloodwork, IV fluids during anesthesia, take-home pain medication, and a follow-up visit. Always ask for an itemized estimate before agreeing to a price.

Location matters too. Metropolitan areas like New York, San Francisco, and Boston typically run 30–50% higher than national averages, while rural areas and the Midwest tend to come in below the median.
